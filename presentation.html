1. Motivation: The Electronic Bottleneck

The Problem

  • Moore's Law is slowing: Transistor scaling is hitting physical thermal limits.
  • AI Energy Cost: Large models consume GWh of electricity.
  • Latency: Electronic inference is bound by clock cycles and memory bandwidth.

The Optical Solution

  • Speed of Light: Propagation performs calculations instantly.
  • Zero Energy: Passive diffraction requires no power consumption for the computation itself.
  • Parallelism: Optics naturally handles massive parallelism (millions of pixels at once).
Proposal Context: Why we are doing this simulation.

2. Simulation Methods (Proposed)

Real-Space (D²NN)

Based on Lin et al. (Science, 2018).

  • Models light propagation between physical planes.
  • Math: Fresnel Propagation (Convolution with free-space kernel).
  • Pros: Physically intuitive (stacked lenses).
  • Cons: Computationally heavy (large convolutions).

Fourier-Space (F-D²NN)

Based on Yan et al. (PRL, 2019).

  • Operates directly in the frequency domain using 4f systems.
  • Math: FFT → Mask → IFFT.
  • Pros: Global receptive field (frequency mixing).
  • Cons: Harder to build physically.
We implemented both architectures in PyTorch to compare them.

3. Where are the Parameters?

In digital networks, weights are dense matrices ($N^2$). In optics, they are diagonal ($N$).

Digital Layer (Dense)

y = W @ x # Matrix Mult # Params: N * N # Mixing: Global

Every input pixel connects to every output pixel via a weight value.

Optical Layer (Diffractive)

y = Mask * x # Element-wise # Params: N (Diagonal) # Mixing: Via Propagation

The "Weight" is the Mask:

  • Phase: Thickness/Refractive Index (Delays light).
  • Amplitude: Opacity (Blocks light).

5. The Curriculum Failure

The Hypothesis

Training on blurry images first (Curriculum Learning) should help convergence, as seen in digital Super-Resolution tasks.

The Result

Validation Accuracy
~10%
With Curriculum

Why it Failed (Physics)

Diffraction is Frequency-Dependent.

  • A lens ground to focus "blobs" (low freq) will scatter "edges" (high freq) incorrectly.
  • By training on blurry images, we trained the physics simulator in a different "universe" than the test set.
  • Fix: Train on sharp images from Epoch 0.

11. Conclusion & Future Work

Conclusions

  • Hybrid is King: Offloading feature extraction to passive optics boosts accuracy (+3.4%) without increasing digital compute load.
  • Curriculum Trap: Optical training requires sharp inputs; domain shift in frequency space is fatal.
  • Fourier vs. Real: Fourier-space models (FD2NN) are more expressive per layer than Real-space (D2NN).

Future Work

  • Physical Deployment: Export learned masks to STL for 3D printing.
  • Energy Profiling: Quantify exact Joule savings of the passive front-end.
  • Complex Datasets: Scale simulation to CIFAR-10 or ImageNet.
